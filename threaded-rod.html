Types of Threaded Rod

As a reliable Threaded Rod Manufacturer, Supplier & Dealer, Klimb Builtech Solutions Pvt. Ltd. offers different types of threaded rods to meet varied industrial and mechanical requirements.

Metric Threaded Rod
Manufactured as per ISO metric standards, these rods are widely used in machinery, structural assembly and industrial installations. They ensure precise fitting with metric nuts and fasteners.
ACME Threaded Rod
Designed with a trapezoidal thread profile, ACME threaded rods are ideal for heavy-load and motion control applications. They are commonly used in lead screws and mechanical drive systems.
Coarse Threaded Rod
Coarse threads have a wider pitch, making installation faster and more durable in construction and heavy-duty environments. They perform well in materials where strong grip is required.
Left-Hand Threaded Rod
These rods tighten in a counterclockwise direction. They are used in specialized machinery applications where normal right-hand threads may loosen due to rotation.
Fine Threaded Rod
Fine threads have a closer pitch, providing better strength and vibration resistance. They are suitable for precision engineering and high-tension fastening applications.

How Threaded Rods Work

A Threaded Rod works on a simple but effective mechanical principle. The threads along the rod create a spiral ridge that allows nuts to move along its length. When tightened, the nut applies clamping force, holding two or more components together securely.

This tightening action converts rotational force into linear pressure. The result is a strong and stable connection that can handle tension and load stress.

The Strength of the Connection Depends On:

  • ➤ Material grade
  • ➤ Thread precision
  • ➤ Diameter of the rod
  • ➤ Proper installation
