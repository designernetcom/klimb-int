What are Mechanical Anchors?

A Mechanical Anchors is a fastening device used to attach structural and non-structural elements to concrete or masonry surfaces. It works through expansion or mechanical interlocking inside the drilled hole, providing strong load-bearing support.

Mechanical Anchors are widely used in:

They are preferred because of their immediate load-carrying capacity and ease of installation.

Machinery installation

How Mechanical Anchors Work

Mechanical anchors work on a simple expansion principle. After drilling a hole in concrete, the anchor is inserted and tightened using a nut or bolt. As it tightens, the anchor expands against the walls of the drilled hole.

This expansion creates:

Frictional resistance

Mechanical interlock

Strong holding force

The tighter the bolt, the stronger the grip - ensuring secure fastening even in heavy-load conditions.

This is why mechanical anchors are widely preferred for structural applications.
